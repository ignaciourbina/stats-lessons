Understanding Bayes' Rule

How to update your beliefs using new evidence

Brief Introduction

Bayes' Rule is one of the most powerful ideas in probability and statistics. It helps you revise what you believe when you encounter new information. In this lesson, you'll explore how Bayes' Rule is derived, how to apply it, and why it's so useful in real-world decision-making. Whether you're dealing with colored balls in boxes or faulty computer chips, this tool will help you think clearly and quantitatively about uncertainty.

Motivating Questions

How do you derive Bayes' Rule from basic probability?
How can you use Bayes' Rule to update your belief in a hypothesis after seeing new evidence?
