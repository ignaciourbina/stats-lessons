Law of Total Probability

Understanding how to compute the probability of an event based on partitions of the sample space.

Brief Introduction

The Law of Total Probability is a fundamental theorem in probability theory that allows us to evaluate the probability of an event by considering several mutually exclusive scenarios that partition the sample space. By breaking down complex problems into simpler components, this law becomes especially useful in real-world applications, such as risk analysis and decision-making processes.
