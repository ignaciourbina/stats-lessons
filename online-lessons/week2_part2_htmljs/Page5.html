Conditional Probability with Tree Diagrams

Using visual aids to understand how conditional probability works

Brief Introduction

Conditional probability is a key concept in statistics that helps you determine the likelihood of an event happening, given that another event has already occurred. In this lesson, you’ll learn how to use tree diagrams to break down and solve these types of problems. By following a real-world example and walking through each step visually and symbolically, you'll gain a deeper and more intuitive understanding of how conditional probability works.

Video Summary

In this video, you’ll learn how to calculate conditional probabilities using tree diagrams. You'll follow a scenario involving John’s commute: he takes the bus 80% of the time, and there's a 60% chance he’s late when he takes the bus. A tree diagram maps out the possible outcomes, helping you see how probabilities branch out. You'll be introduced to the conditional probability formula, P(A ∥ B) = P(A and B) / P(B), and use it to compute the likelihood of John being late given that he took the bus—resulting in a probability of 0.75. The video guides you through both symbolic and intuitive explanations, helping you become comfortable with the concept. You'll also apply the formula to other scenarios, such as finding the probability of not being late if John didn’t take the bus.
