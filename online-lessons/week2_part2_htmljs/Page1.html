Reinforcing key ideas of conditional probability with a practical cricket example

Brief Introduction

In this lesson, we revisit the concept of conditional probability to strengthen your understanding of how prior events influence the likelihood of outcomes. Building on our earlier introduction, we now explore the idea through a detailed real-world case—using cricket performance statistics to see conditional probability in action. This review is designed to clarify the concept and emphasize the contrast between conditional and unconditional probabilities.

Video Summary

This video reinforces the core idea of conditional probability using Cheteshwar Pujara’s cricket stats as a case study. You’ve already encountered the definition: the likelihood of event A occurring given that event B has happened, written as P(A ∥ B). Here, we look at how often Pujara converts fifties into centuries—16 out of 31 times—compared to his overall century rate of 16 out of 76 innings. This clear contrast (52% vs. 21%) vividly illustrates how conditioning changes probabilities. The video walks through this reasoning and concludes with the standard formula: P(B ∥ A) = P(A ∩ B) / P(A). It’s a focused and practical review to solidify your grasp of the concept before moving on to more formal tools and techniques.
