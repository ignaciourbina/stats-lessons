Unions, Intersections, and Complements in Probability

Applying set theory to understand compound probability events

Brief Introduction

Probability often involves more than one event. This lesson focuses on how we can use unions, intersections, and complements to describe and calculate probabilities involving combinations of events. Through set notation, Venn diagrams, and relatable examples, you'll gain tools to analyze scenarios like “either-or,” “both,” or “neither” outcomes with clarity and confidence.
