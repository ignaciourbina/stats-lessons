Chi-square Analysis and Frequencies

You suspect that a six-sided dice might be biased and decide to test your theory by rolling it 120 times. The observed frequencies for the faces from 1 to 6 are 22, 18, 20, 15, 20, and 25, respectively. Assuming that in a fair face the probability of observing each face is 1/6, you perform a Chi-square test at the 0.05 significance level.
