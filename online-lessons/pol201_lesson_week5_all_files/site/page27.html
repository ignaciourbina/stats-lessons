Chi-square Analysis and Frequencies

Analysis of Dice Fairness

The following results are based on a Chi-square test conducted to determine if a six-sided dice is fair based on 120 rolls.

Steps to Solve the Question:

  1. Calculate the expected frequencies assuming the dice is fair (each face should appear 20 times).
  2. Use the observed frequencies of each face from the game.
  3. Compute the Chi-square statistic to compare observed and expected frequencies. Note that we have 6 frequency cells, so we should use (6 - 1) = 5 degrees of freedom for the Chi-sq distribution.
  4. Calculate the p-value to assess the significance of the results. In this case, p-value = Pr( X â‰¥ {Chi.sq Statistic} ).

Results:

Statistic Value
Chi-square Statistic 2.9
P-Value 0.715

Based on the analysis, the p-value of 0.715 is greater than the typical alpha level of 0.05, suggesting no statistically significant evidence to indicate the dice is biased.

The formula for the Chi-square statistic is:

\[ \chi^2 \text{ = } \sum \frac{ \text{($O_i$ - $E_i$)}^2}{E_i} \]

where \( O_i \) is the observed frequency, and \( E_i \) is the expected frequency.
